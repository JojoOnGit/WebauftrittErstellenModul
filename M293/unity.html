Album Info

Unity (2024)

Released in March 2024, "Unity" is the breakthrough album from Dutch artist Joost Klein. The album gained international attention when its lead single "Europapa" was selected as the Netherlands' entry for Eurovision 2024.

The album blends genres including Europop, dance, electronic, and hip-hop, while incorporating multilingual lyrics in Dutch, English, and French.

My Review

When I first discovered Joost Klein's "Unity," I wasn't sure what to expect. I'd heard "Europapa" from Eurovision, but wasn't prepared for how the entire album would completely win me over. There's something so refreshingly authentic about Klein's approach to music - it's playful, energetic, and utterly infectious.

The album perfectly captures that feeling of pure joy that seems increasingly rare in modern music. Tracks like "1" and "Gabberland" are impossible not to move to, while "Discozwemmen" showcases a more thoughtful side to his artistry without losing the album's vibrant energy.

What I love most about this record is how effortlessly it blends languages and cultures. Klein switches between Dutch, English, and French, creating this wonderfully inclusive vibe that feels like a celebration of European unity (fitting with both the album title and his Eurovision connection). In our increasingly divided world, there's something powerful about music that brings people together through shared joy.

The production is brilliantly nostalgic too - calling back to 90s Eurodance but with enough modern elements to feel fresh rather than derivative. I find myself returning to this album whenever I need a mood boost - it's practically impossible to feel down while listening to it.

If you haven't given "Unity" a chance yet, especially if you dismissed it as just "that Eurovision album," I highly recommend diving in. It's easily one of my most played albums of 2024, and for good reason - pure, unadulterated musical joy.
